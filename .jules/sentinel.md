## 2025-06-11 - Upgrade Scope IDs from Predictable Doubles to Cryptographically Secure GUIDs
**Vulnerability:** The lock scopes and reaction contexts within `MemoizR` relied on `Random.Shared.NextDouble()` for generating identifiers. This is not cryptographically secure and could lead to scope ID collisions across parallel evaluations or contexts, especially in highly concurrent applications.
**Learning:** For tracking logical async flows and managing scope/locks in reentrant patterns where threads frequently hop across `await`, identifiers must be mathematically guaranteed to be unique. Using predictable or pseudo-random values (like `double` from `Random`) risks "collisions" where independent operations incorrectly assume they are in the same scope, breaking reentrancy contracts and potentially deadlocking or granting unauthorized execution inside exclusive locks.
**Prevention:** Always use cryptographically secure unique identifiers (e.g., `Guid.NewGuid()`) for cross-context or cross-thread request/scope tracking instead of weak pseudo-random number generators.
