## 2024-06-13 - MemoHandlR Hot Path LINQ Allocations
**Learning:** In the MemoizR dependency graph, methods like `AddObserver`, `RemoveObserver`, and `UpdateSourceAndObserverLinks` execute on extremely hot paths during graph invalidation and re-evaluation. Using LINQ (`.Take()`, `.Where()`, `.Contains()`) and collection expressions (`[.. x]`) on the `Sources` and `Observers` arrays causes severe enumerator and interface dispatch allocations, dragging down performance and triggering premature GC sweeps of `WeakReference` lists. Filtering arrays of `WeakReference` with two-pass logic (like LINQ's `.Where()` or array comprehensions) risks index-out-of-bounds errors or missed sweeps if a GC runs between the element-count sizing pass and the population pass.
**Action:** Replaced LINQ and collection expressions in `MemoHandlR.cs` with direct `Array.Copy()`, manual `for` loops, and `Array.IndexOf()`. Use a single-pass temporary array for sweeping `WeakReference` lists to ensure GC-safe filtering while maintaining maximum iteration performance.
